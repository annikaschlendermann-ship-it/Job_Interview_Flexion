My background is in Software Engineering, Data & AI, and ML recruiting. The robotics and RL domain is new territory — though my approach of learning the technical context before engaging candidates has served me well across disciplines. The sourcing and process foundations transfer well.

/004 First 90 Days / ▾

How I’d build
Flexion’s hiring engine.

Here is how I would approach the first three months at Flexion.

Days 1–30
Listen & Map
  • Deep-dive intake with every hiring manager — understand the technical problem space, not just the job spec
  • Audit the current candidate flow: where it breaks, where it’s absent
  • Map the competitive landscape: who else is hiring in RL, control systems, and robotics in Europe
  • Set up or audit the ATS — clean data, clear stages, GDPR compliance from day one
  • Build the first KPI baseline: time-to-hire, funnel shape, inbound vs. sourced ratio
Days 31–60
Build the Pipeline
  • Launch proactive sourcing for the two most critical open roles — robotics engineering, applied ML
  • Begin outreach to target researchers at ETH Zürich, TU Munich, Imperial College, and relevant labs
  • Set up structured referral mechanics within the existing team
  • Begin Flexion employer brand audit: social presence, LinkedIn positioning, messaging consistency
  • First pipeline health report to founders: where we are, where we’re going
Days 61–90
Deliver & Design
  • First offers closed — end-to-end from intro call through negotiation
  • Defined interview process for core technical roles: structured, consistent, fast
  • Flexion employer brand strategy v1: where we show up, how we tell the story
  • Onboarding handover protocol so no candidate drops post-offer
  • 90-day retro with founders: what’s working, what to double down on
